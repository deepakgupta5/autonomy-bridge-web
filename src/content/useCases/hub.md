## Warehouse Automation Use Cases
Use Cases are structured decision-framework evaluations applying Autonomy Bridge analytical models to specific warehouse automation investment problems. Each use case defines the operational context, the decision problem, the analytical approach, the key variables, and the economic logic that determines whether the investment is viable.

Use cases are not vendor case studies. They are operator-side analyses of the conditions under which automation investments succeed or fail — built to help operators, investors, and advisors evaluate automation decisions using the same analytical framework regardless of which vendor or system is under consideration.

## Use Case Index
### [AMR Deployment Evaluation](/use-cases/amr-deployment-evaluation)

**Framework:** [Workflow Architecture Framework](/frameworks/workflow-architecture-framework) · [Robotics ROI Model](/frameworks/robotics-roi-model)

Evaluates whether autonomous mobile robots should be deployed to improve warehouse productivity. The central variable is task density: AMR economics collapse when transport demand is intermittent. Covers fleet sizing, utilization modeling, congestion risk, and the economic conditions under which travel labor removal justifies the capital commitment.

---

### [Warehouse Automation ROI Evaluation](/use-cases/warehouse-automation-roi-evaluation)

**Framework:** [Robotics ROI Model](/frameworks/robotics-roi-model) · [Automation Failure Framework](/frameworks/automation-failure-framework)

Evaluates whether automation investment can be recovered across the full demand cycle of a 3PL distribution facility. The central variable is utilization stability: automation converts variable labor into fixed infrastructure, and capital recovery fails when utilization falls below the minimum threshold. Covers demand variability, labor substitution, workflow bottlenecks, and the capital recovery threshold.

---

### [Goods-to-Person System Evaluation](/use-cases/goods-to-person-system-evaluation)

**Framework:** [Warehouse Automation Decision Framework](/frameworks/warehouse-automation-decision-framework) · [Workflow Architecture Framework](/frameworks/workflow-architecture-framework)

Evaluates whether a goods-to-person system improves overall facility throughput or shifts the bottleneck downstream. The central variable is end-to-end workflow capacity: improving pick rate without addressing packing, sortation, and outbound constraints does not increase shipped order volume. Covers pick station throughput, downstream capacity, demand stability, and capital intensity.

---

### [Robotics Deployment in 3PL Warehouses](/use-cases/robotics-deployment-3pl-warehouses)

**Framework:** [Robotics ROI Model](/frameworks/robotics-roi-model) · [Automation Failure Framework](/frameworks/automation-failure-framework)

Evaluates the specific risks of deploying robotics automation in multi-client 3PL facilities. The central variable is client demand stability: 3PL operators do not control their own order volume, and automation installed for one large account becomes underutilized if that client reduces volume or exits. Covers client portfolio concentration, utilization risk, and the demand validation required before capital commitment.

## How Use Cases Connect to Frameworks
Each use case applies one or more Autonomy Bridge frameworks as its analytical foundation. The [Robotics ROI Model](/frameworks/robotics-roi-model) governs economic evaluation across all four use cases. The [Workflow Architecture Framework](/frameworks/workflow-architecture-framework) provides the constraint analysis methodology for AMR and goods-to-person evaluations. The [Automation Failure Framework](/frameworks/automation-failure-framework) identifies the structural failure modes relevant to ROI and 3PL deployment contexts.

Glossary terms referenced across these use cases — including [Removable Labor Share](/glossary/removable-labor-share), [Capital Recovery Period](/glossary/capital-recovery-period), [Peak-to-Average Ratio](/glossary/peak-to-average-ratio), and [Workflow Constraint](/glossary/workflow-constraint) — are defined in the [Glossary](/glossary).

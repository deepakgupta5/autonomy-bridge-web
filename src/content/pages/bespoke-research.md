## Bespoke Research
A bespoke research engagement produces one thing: a decision-grade output built for a specific question, operator context, or investment thesis — not a literature review, not a market survey, not a recycled category report. Every engagement begins with a defined decision problem and ends with a structured research product that maps the competitive position, deployment risk, vendor economics, or automation ROI of a specific scenario. The analysis draws on primary source triangulation, operator-level data, and the Autonomy Bridge framework set. The deliverable is ready to act on.

## Research Types
### Market Entry Analysis
A structured assessment of addressable opportunity, competitive density, and entry constraint for a defined automation segment or geography. Outputs include demand segmentation, removable labor share modeling, and a ranked entry sequence with risk-adjusted rationale. See: [Removable Labor Share →](/glossary/removable-labor-share)

### Vendor Economics Assessment
A bottom-up analysis of vendor unit economics, pricing architecture, and margin sustainability across a competitive set. Applied where procurement teams or investors need to understand whether a vendor's commercial model survives at scale — not just whether the technology works. See: [Vendor Economics →](/glossary/vendor-economics)

### Deployment Risk Report
A diagnostic of the operational, integration, and financial failure modes in a specific automation deployment scenario. Covers pilot-to-scale failure vectors, utilization modeling gaps, and the conditions under which a deployment stalls, reverses, or destroys ROI. See: [Ramp Risk →](/glossary/ramp-risk)

### Automation ROI Model
A structured financial model mapping labor displacement, throughput uplift, integration cost, and payback period for a defined deployment context. Built on operator-specific inputs, not industry averages. Output includes scenario variants and a sensitivity table calibrated to the client's cost structure.

## What You Get
- A structured research product delivered as a formal document: executive summary, findings, framework analysis, and recommendation layer — formatted for board, investment committee, or executive team use

## Process
### Step 1 — Brief
Submit a research question or decision problem via the contact form. The brief should describe the decision to be made, the context (operator type, geography, technology category), and any constraints on scope or timeline.

### Step 2 — Scoping
Autonomy Bridge returns a scoping document within 48 hours. It defines the research question precisely, identifies the output type, maps the data sources and framework references to be applied, and confirms the fee and delivery date.

### Step 3 — Research
Primary source collection, operator and market data triangulation, and framework-driven analysis. All claims are sourced and graded. Where quantitative modeling is required, assumptions are documented and validated against comparable deployment data.

### Step 4 — Delivery
The completed research product is delivered as a structured document on the confirmed date. A 30-minute delivery briefing is included to walk through findings, flag limitations, and address immediate questions.

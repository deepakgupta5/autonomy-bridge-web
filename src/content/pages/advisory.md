## Advisory
## Engagement for AI, Robotics, and Industrial Automation Markets

Most consultants arrive with a methodology and map your problem to it. Autonomy Bridge works the other way: the engagement starts with a diagnostic of where your commercial or analytical constraint actually sits, and builds outward from there. The work draws on primary market research, proprietary frameworks across vendor economics, automation ROI, and workflow architecture, and direct operational experience across 3PL deployment, autonomous systems, and industrial automation in North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific. Engagements are scoped to produce decisions, not decks.

## Who This Is For
**Warehouse Operators and 3PL Executives**
You are evaluating automation vendors or managing a pilot that hasn't converted to full deployment. The constraint is usually not technology — it's the absence of an economic model that accounts for your actual throughput variability, labor mix, and lease structure. We build that model and translate it into vendor selection criteria or deployment sequencing. See: [Capital Recovery Period →](/glossary/capital-recovery-period)

**Logistics and Industrial Automation Investors**
You are conducting due diligence on a robotics vendor, an autonomous systems company, or a distribution infrastructure asset. You need a market-grounded view of unit economics, competitive positioning, and the realistic path to scaled deployment. We run the research and stress-test the assumptions in the materials you've been given. See: [Vendor Economics →](/glossary/vendor-economics)

**Robotics and Automation Vendors**
You are entering a new vertical or segment and lack the buyer-side operational context to position effectively. Your sales cycle is longer than it should be because your team is selling capability, not quantified outcomes. We diagnose the positioning gap and produce the market and buyer intelligence needed to close it. See: [Workflow Constraint →](/glossary/workflow-constraint)

## How We Work
Autonomy Bridge engagements run on a monthly retainer or a project basis. Retainer engagements are structured for continuity: standing access to research, iterative output across a defined scope, and a consistent analytical perspective on commercial decisions as they develop. This model works best when you have ongoing decisions to make — vendor evaluation cycles, market entry planning, or sustained sales and pricing strategy work.

Project-based engagements are scoped to a defined output: a market sizing model, a vendor economics analysis, a workflow constraint diagnostic, or a competitive intelligence report. Timeline and deliverables are fixed at the outset. These are appropriate when you have a specific decision to support and a clear analytical gap to fill.

Retainer and project engagements can be combined. A project-based diagnostic often reveals a broader set of strategic questions; a retainer can follow from that foundation. Scope is always defined in writing before any work begins.

## What We Don't Do
- **Implementation.** We provide analysis, frameworks, and commercial strategy. We do not manage deployments, build software, or staff embedded operations roles.
- **General management consulting.** We work in a defined domain: AI, robotics, and industrial automation markets. Engagements outside that domain are declined.
- **Speculative retainers.** We don't take open-ended engagements without a defined scope. If there isn't a clear analytical or commercial constraint to work on, the timing isn't right.

## Typical Engagement Scope
- **Warehouse automation utilization modeling** — capacity and throughput analysis under variable demand, mapped against automation system specifications and labor substitution economics
- **Vendor economics analysis** — total cost of ownership across competing automation platforms, normalized for deployment environment, integration complexity, and maintenance burden
- **Workflow constraint mapping** — identification of operational bottlenecks limiting automation ROI, with quantified impact on throughput, labor cost, and order cycle time
- **Market entry and segment sizing** — addressable market analysis for a specific automation technology or deployment segment, with buyer profile segmentation and competitive positioning assessment
- **Commercial diagnostic for robotics vendors** — audit of sales cycle performance, win/loss patterns, and positioning gaps, with go-to-market recommendations grounded in buyer-side operational context

## Frequently Asked Questions
**What types of engagements does Autonomy Bridge offer?**
Autonomy Bridge offers two engagement structures: monthly retainer and project-based. Retainer engagements provide standing access to research and a consistent analytical perspective across ongoing commercial decisions — vendor evaluation cycles, market entry planning, or sustained pricing strategy work. Project-based engagements are scoped to a single defined output — a market sizing model, a vendor economics analysis, a workflow constraint diagnostic, or a competitive intelligence report — with fixed timeline and deliverables. The two models can be combined when a project diagnostic reveals a broader set of strategic questions requiring sustained support.

**Who are Autonomy Bridge's advisory clients?**
Autonomy Bridge serves three client types. Warehouse operators and 3PL executives evaluating automation investments or managing pilots that have not converted to full deployment — the work builds the economic model that accounts for their actual throughput variability, labor mix, and lease structure. Logistics and industrial automation investors conducting due diligence on robotics vendors, autonomous systems companies, or distribution infrastructure assets — the work provides a market-grounded view of unit economics and stresses the assumptions in vendor materials. Robotics and automation vendors entering new verticals or segments who need buyer-side operational context to close positioning gaps and shorten sales cycles.

**What does Autonomy Bridge not do?**
Autonomy Bridge does not manage deployments, build software, or staff embedded operations roles — the work is analytical and strategic, not implementation. Engagements outside the defined domain of AI, robotics, and industrial automation markets are declined. Open-ended retainers without a defined scope are not accepted: if there is no clear analytical or commercial constraint to work on, the engagement is not the right fit. These boundaries are structural — they preserve the analytical quality and domain focus that make the work useful.

**How is an Autonomy Bridge engagement scoped?**
Every engagement scope is defined in writing before work begins. Scope definition starts with a diagnostic of where the commercial or analytical constraint actually sits — not a fixed methodology applied to every client. For project-based engagements, the output, timeline, and deliverables are fixed at the outset. For retainer engagements, the recurring scope is defined by the decision cycle being supported. A scope conversation typically takes one call; the written scope document follows before any commercial commitment is made.

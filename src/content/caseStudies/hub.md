## Case Studies — Autonomy Bridge
Five anonymised consulting engagements drawn from Deepak Gupta's work across 3PL operators, industrial technology companies, private equity firms, and warehouse robotics vendors. All client names, specific financials, and identifying details are anonymised or composited. Each study follows the problem → approach → output → outcome structure and maps to one or more Autonomy Bridge frameworks.

## Case Study Index
### [Automation Investment Screening for a 3PL Operator](/case-studies/automation-deployment-risk-assessment)

**Client type:** Mid-size 3PL fulfillment operator
**Frameworks:** [Robotics ROI Model](/frameworks/robotics-roi-model) · [Automation Failure Framework](/frameworks/automation-failure-framework)

A regional 3PL operator needed an independent assessment of a proposed goods-to-person automation investment before committing capital. Vendor ROI assumptions relied on peak-period utilization that the facility's demand cycle could not sustain. The engagement modeled utilization across three demand scenarios and identified a decision boundary below which the investment did not recover within the vendor's projected payback period.

---

### [Robotics Market Entry Decision Analysis](/case-studies/robotics-market-entry-decision-analysis)

**Client type:** Industrial technology company
**Frameworks:** [Warehouse Automation Decision Framework](/frameworks/warehouse-automation-decision-framework) · [Vendor Economics Framework](/frameworks/vendor-economics-framework)

A mid-size industrial automation company evaluated whether to enter the North American warehouse robotics market through organic development or acquisition. The engagement structured the operator decision logic — the utilization-driven economics that govern automation capital approval — and screened two acquisition candidates against defined commercial and financial criteria.

---

### [AI and Robotics Market Sizing for Private Equity](/case-studies/ai-robotics-market-sizing-engagement)

**Client type:** Private equity firm, mid-market industrial technology focus
**Frameworks:** [Robotics ROI Model](/frameworks/robotics-roi-model) · [Warehouse Automation Decision Framework](/frameworks/warehouse-automation-decision-framework)

A PE firm required a market sizing model grounded in real warehouse deployment economics rather than theoretical robotics adoption potential. The engagement distinguished the economically viable addressable market — facilities where automation can sustain utilization above the capital recovery threshold — from the theoretical total addressable market used in vendor materials.

---

### [Vendor Deployment Viability Assessment](/case-studies/vendor-deployment-viability-assessment)

**Client type:** Warehouse robotics vendor, Series B stage
**Frameworks:** [Pilot-to-Scale Failure Framework](/frameworks/pilot-to-scale-failure-framework) · [Vendor Evaluation Framework](/frameworks/vendor-evaluation-framework)

A venture-backed robotics company was experiencing stalled deployments — pilots that completed without converting to full facility commitments. The engagement identified that stalls occurred consistently at the capital approval gate, not the operational evaluation stage, because vendor proposals lacked utilization modeling across full demand cycles, disaggregated labor substitution estimates, and downside scenario analysis.

---

### [Robotics Pricing Strategy Research](/case-studies/robotics-pricing-strategy-research)

**Client type:** Warehouse robotics vendor
**Frameworks:** [Vendor Economics Framework](/frameworks/vendor-economics-framework)

A robotics vendor evaluated how different pricing structures — capital purchase, subscription-based robotics-as-a-service, and usage-based pricing — affected operator adoption decisions. The engagement analyzed how each pricing model shifted financial risk between vendor and operator under different utilization and demand stability conditions.
